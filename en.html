Research

Research practice centered on stable reasoning and interpretable results

Jan 2026 - Present Research Intern · JLU AVC Lab

Emotion Recognition with Reinforcement Learning and Chain-of-Thought Optimization

To address hallucinated reasoning and unstable logic when audio is missing, I proposed a GRPO-based reinforcement learning framework. The method explicitly segments episode-level reasoning chains and combines acoustic and semantic scoring models to build a semantic reward mechanism.

  • Separated visual guesswork from unsupported hallucinations to enable cross-modal semantic calibration
  • Introduced perturbations and hidden-state stability rewards for token-level reward allocation
  • Provided positive feedback when the reasoning logic was sound even if the final answer was wrong, improving generalization

I lead research on an explainable zero-shot learning model aimed at recognizing unseen vulnerabilities and improving interpretability in multi-label software vulnerability detection. My responsibilities include literature review, solution design, and model development.
